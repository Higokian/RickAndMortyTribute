The History of Rick and Morty

Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon, the creators of Rick and Morty, first met at Channel 101. Channel 101 is a monthly Las Angeles short film festival that started back in 2004. Previously a producer, Roiland began submitting his “sick and twisted” content in 2005. Harmon enjoyed Roilands content, so the duo started collaborating. After getting fired from a television series in 2006, Roiland directed his talents to submitting a webisode, The Real Animated Adventures of Doc and Mharti, to Channel 101. In 2012, Harmon asked Roiland for ideas on an animated series. Roiland responded with the idea of using the characters from Doc and Mharti, but renaming them to Rick and Morty. Initially, Roiland anticipated an eleven-minute segment, but Adult Swim wanted it to be a thirty-minute segment. Due to the substantially longer segment, Harmon thought it best to create a family around Rick and Morty. This lead to the debut of the first season December 2, 2013.
